Early Life and Career

Billy Blanks Jr. was born in 1979 to fitness guru Billy Blanks and his wife, Gayle H. Godfrey. From a young age, he had a passion for dancing and was mentored by the renowned Paula Abdul. Recognizing that his father’s workout programs were too challenging for many individuals, Blanks Jr. embarked on a mission to create a fitness program that was accessible to everyone. This inspired him to develop the “Dance it Out” program, which combines fitness and dance in a fun and engaging way.

However, Blanks Jr.’s journey to success was not without its struggles. At one point, he faced the possibility of homelessness. Determined to pursue his dreams, he worked tirelessly as a professional dancer, appearing in music videos and even touring with the musical “Fame.”

Blanks Jr. and his wife also ventured into the fitness DVD market, but encountered difficulties with their initial releases. It wasn’t until his appearance on the reality TV show “Shark Tank” that his “Dance it Out” program gained significant popularity and success. This breakthrough propelled him to achieve his well-deserved recognition and acclaim.
