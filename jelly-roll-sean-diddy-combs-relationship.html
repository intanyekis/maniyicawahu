Jelly Roll Refused To Meet Sean 'Diddy' Combs After Getting A Bad Feeling About The Controversial Ra

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, September 6, 2024

Highlights

  • Jelly Roll refused to meet Diddy due to a bad feeling he got, leading him to bail on the encounter abruptly.
  • Jelly Roll joked about Diddy's connection to Tupac's death.
  • Jelly Roll, known for liking photos with celebrities, decided he didn't even want a picture with Diddy.

After becoming one of the most successful music producers in history, Sean "Diddy" Combs was a powerbroker for years. That amount of power allowed Diddy to raise lots of kids, employ people who've spoken out about him, and have several famous friends.

After seeming on top of the world for years, Diddy then went on to face accusations that led to his home being raided. Before that controversy, Jelly Roll became successful enough, thanks to his wife helping to launch his career, that he got the chance to meet Diddy, but he refused to.

This article will reveal what Jelly Roll said about refusing to meet Diddy because he had a bad feeling about the rapper. Then, the article will look at the fact that it seems like Jelly Roll loves meeting celebrities.

Jelly Roll Bailed On Sean "Diddy" Combs Because He Felt Like Something Was Wrong With The Rapper

In April 2024, the country singer and songwriter Jelly Roll appeared on the "CANCELLED with Tana Mongeau" podcast. During the resulting conversation, Jelly Roll revealed many interesting stories, including the time he met Taylor Swift.

Even though millions of people are fascinated by stories about Swift, another one of Jelly Roll's anecdotes got a lot more attention. Instead, people were a lot more interested in the fact that Jelly Roll told Mongeau that he bailed on meeting Sean "Diddy" Combs.

"This is the first time in my career, ever, where they said, 'Do you want to meet such-and-such?' And I said, 'Yeah,' and I started walking that way, and as I was getting down the hallway — this is a true story — I said, 'Nah,' and went and got back in the car."

As Jelly Roll explained during his podcast appearance, he got the chance to meet Diddy when they appeared on Jimmy Kimmel Live! the same day.

Which stars wanted nothing to do with Diddy, according to The Things?

  • 50 Cent

After Jelly Roll told Mongeau that he refused to meet Diddy, he began to try to explain the reasoning behind his decision. However, Jelly Roll admitted that he couldn't put his finger on the exact reason why he didn't want to meet the famous rapper. "I don't know what it was."

However, Jelly Roll was able to describe the moment that cemented his opinion that he didn't want to interact with Diddy. According to Jelly Roll, he tried to make a joke and decided that he didn't want to have anything to do with Diddy based on how the rapper's people reacted.

"I made a joke at first. I was like, 'Who don't wanna meet the guy that got Tupac killed?' And nobody thought that was funny. So, I was like, 'Oh, that's a bomb. Maybe I shouldn't go.'"

Jelly Roll went on to explain how Diddy's people reacted to his joke. Even though he thought he would get a laugh, Jelly Roll got a warning about making a joke about Tupac around Diddy instead.

"I was already skeptical because I thought I had a funny one and they were like, "I probably wouldn't say that.' You know what I mean?"

Jelly Roll is humbled by fans who sing his songs and attend his concerts, but one fan interaction is especially memorable for him.

As Jelly Roll was walking down a hall toward Diddy's green room after making that joke, he got a really bed feeling about the idea of meeting the rapper. In fact, Jelly Roll claimed during the podcast that he quickly concluded that he didn't even want to be pictured with Diddy.

"When we were walking, I was just like, 'I don't know.' It's just like, very seldom does things rub me in a way where I was like, 'I don't even know if that's a picture I want.'"

Unfortunately for Jelly Roll, it turns out that he has a history of being photographed with people he doesn't want to be associated with.

When Jelly Roll appeared on Tana Mongeau's podcast, he was accompanied by his wife Alyssa "BunnieXo" DeFord. From the second Jelly Roll brought Diddy up, BunnieXo made her feelings about the rapper clear by giving him an insulting nickname.

As Jelly Roll explained during his appearance on Mongeau's podcast, sometimes stars are just shoved together for photograph opportunities. Jelly Roll sadly has regrets about some photos of him that were taken in situations like that.

"Because, I've been in situations where, and I won't say this stuff, where I've, like, ended up in pictures with people I didn't want to be with. Just like, just because, you know, motherf**kers will walk a carpet around the same time and s**t happens. And they be like, 'Hey, y'all get together. And you're like, 'Yo, I don't really even know who this human is.'"

Jelly Roll Has A Well-Documented Love For Having His Photo Taken With Celebrities

One of the keys to the success that Jelly Roll has enjoyed is that fans find him likable. After all, Jelly Roll comes across as a regular guy who happens to have musical talent and a unique look.

While there are many reasons why Jelly Roll seems like a regular guy, the fact that he seems to be excited whenever he meets celebrities is one of them. Just like most regular people, Jelly Roll seems to love having his photo taken with celebrities.

A perfect example of Jelly Roll acting like a fanboy took place when he got to attend the Grammy Awards in February 2024.

After the Grammy Awards ended, it was very clear that Jelly Roll got to rub elbows with many stars at the event. The reason for that is that Jelly Roll posed for photos with many celebrities at the event, and he seemed delighted in the resulting images.

Some of the celebrities Jelly Roll was photographed at the Grammys with include Kelly Clarkson, Taylor Swift, Shania Twain, Paul McCartney, Jon Bon Jovi, Ed Sheeran, and Jay-Z. Considering Jelly Roll seemed overjoyed to be photographed with those stars, his comment about not wanting to be in a picture with Diddy means even more.
