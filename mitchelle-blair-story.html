Mother Who Tortured Her Kids &amp; Put Them in Freezer

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, August 24, 2024

In a chilling and horrifying case that shocked the nation, Mitchelle Blair, a mother of four from Detroit, Michigan, murdered two of her own children and kept their bodies in a freezer for almost three years.

The gruesome discovery was made on March 24, 2015, after the 35-year-old mother was evicted from her apartment for failing to pay rent.

Crews from the 36th District Court were sent to remove her belongings when they stumbled upon the deep freezer containing the remains of her two children, Stephen Berry and Stoni Blair.

This terrifying story serves as a stark reminder of the unimaginable horrors that can occur behind closed doors, even within a family.

The Discovery of the Bodies

The tragic story began when Mitchelle Blair was served an eviction notice for owing $2,206 in unpaid rent to her apartment complex.

On the morning of March 24, 2015, a crew from the 36th District Court arrived at her apartment to remove furniture and other belongings. It was during this process that they found the deep freezer in the living room, which held a horrifying secret.

Inside the freezer, the crew discovered the body of a teenage girl wrapped in a plastic bag, and underneath her was the body of a younger boy.

The police were immediately called, and they soon identified the bodies as Blair’s own children, Stephen Berry and Stoni Blair. The medical examiner later determined that the children had been dead for at least three years and ruled their deaths as homicides.

Upon arresting Mitchelle Blair, she reportedly proclaimed, “I’m sorry.” This would be the beginning of an investigation that would uncover the dark and disturbing truth behind the deaths of Stephen and Stoni.

The Trial of Mitchelle Blair

Mitchelle Blair was arrested and brought to trial, where she confessed to the murders of her children, Stephen and Stoni. She claimed that she killed her “demons” because they were sexually abusing her youngest son, Matthew – an accusation that has never been substantiated.

In August 2012, Blair discovered her son Matthew simulating sexual activity with dolls. When she asked him why he was doing that and if anyone had done that to him, he revealed that his brother, Stephen, had abused him.

Blair then confronted Stephen, who also confessed to the abuse. In a fit of rage, she started punching and kicking him, eventually placing a garbage bag over his head until he lost consciousness.

Blair admitted to torturing Stephen by pouring scalding hot water on his genitals, causing his skin to peel off, and making him drink Windex. She also wrapped a belt around his neck, lifted him up, and asked him if he liked the feeling of being choked. Stephen lost consciousness once more and eventually died from the abuse.

Nine months later, Blair found out that her daughter Stoni was also sexually abusing Matthew. As a result, she began to starve Stoni and brutally beat her until she died in May 2013.

Blair initially wanted to turn herself into the police, but after Matthew told her he didn’t want her to go, she decided to put Stoni’s body in a plastic bag and stuff her in the deep freezer on top of Stephen’s body.

Throughout the trial, Blair showed no remorse for her actions. In court, she even told the judge, “I would kill them again.”

In June 2015, she pleaded guilty to two counts of first-degree premeditated murder and was sentenced to life in prison. She is currently serving her sentence at the Huron Valley Correctional Facility in Ypsilanti, Michigan.

The chilling story of Mitchelle Blair and her crimes has attracted widespread attention and has been featured in Investigation Discovery’s true-crime series, Evil Lives Here.

The episode, titled “I Made It Out Alive,” aired on January 29, 2023, and shed light on the horrific events that took place within the Blair household.

The synopsis of the episode reads, “For years, every time Gabi Blair walks past the freezer, she shudders at the thought of the horrors hidden inside; forced to keep secret her mother’s crimes, she prays that someone will discover the truth before she becomes her mother’s next victim.”

This episode not only focuses on the gruesome details of the murders committed by Mitchelle Blair but also delves into the psychological effects her actions had on her surviving children.

The program aims to raise awareness about the potential dangers lurking within seemingly ordinary families and serves as a reminder of the importance of providing support and resources for struggling parents.

Moreover, the series highlights the need for increased vigilance in protecting vulnerable children from abuse and emphasizes the role that society can play in identifying and addressing such cases before they escalate to tragedy.
