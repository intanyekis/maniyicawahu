Who Is Ravi Shastri?

Ravi Shankar Young:- Ravi Shankar Jayadrith Shastri is a former Indian national cricket team head coach, cricket pundit, and former captain of the Indian cricket team. He was a member of the Indian national cricket team from 1981 to 1992, appearing in both Test matches and One Day Internationals. He started his career as a left-arm spin bowler but later developed into a batting all-rounder.

His professional high point came in 1985 when he was named Champion of Champions at the World Championship of Cricket in Australia. In the same season, on 10 January 1985, he equaled West Indian Gary Sobers' first-class record of six sixes in an over. He was considered a potential captain, but due to his reputation outside cricket, injuries, and a tendency to lose form at crucial moments, he captained India in only one Test match.
