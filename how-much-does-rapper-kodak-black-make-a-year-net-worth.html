How Much Does Rapper Kodak Black Make A Year? The Truth About His Mysterious Net Worth

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, September 1, 2024

Kodak Black is one of the most controversial and enigmatic rappers in the music industry today. He has become a household name for his unique style, hard-hitting lyrics, and his run-ins with the law. However, one question that has been on everyone's mind is how much money does Kodak Black make a year? The answer to this question has been shrouded in mystery for years, with various sources reporting different figures.

The sources and accuracy of reported income for Kodak Black have been a topic of much debate and speculation among fans and critics alike. While some claim that the rapper's net worth is in the millions, others argue that he is not nearly as wealthy as he appears to be.

Here's the truth about the famous Florida rapper and his net worth.

What Is Kodak Black Known For?

Kodak Black was born on June 11, 1997, in Pompano Beach, Florida. Growing up in a public housing project called the Golden Acres, Kodak Black had a tough childhood. According to The FADER, his mother was an immigrant from Haiti and his father was absent for most of his life.

Kodak's early life was marred by poverty and crime. He started writing rap lyrics at a young age to cope with the harsh realities of his surroundings. He would wind up becoming friends with future NFL superstars Antonio Brown and Lamar Jackson at this time, the latter of whom would help get Kodak pardoned in 2021.

At the age of twelve, Kodak Black joined a local rap group called Brutal Youngnz. The group performed at local events and parties but disbanded after some members were arrested for robbery. Despite this setback, Kodak Black continued to pursue music as a career and started releasing mixtapes under the name J-Black.

In 2013, Kodak Black released his first mixtape titled "Project Baby" which gained popularity on social media platforms like YouTube and SoundCloud. This success caught the attention of rapper Drake who posted a video dancing to one of Kodak's songs on Instagram. This wouldn't be the first time Drake has worked with an up-and-coming celebrity.

In 2015, Kodak Black signed to Atlantic Records and released his fourth mixtape "Institution" which featured hits like "No Flockin" and "SKRT". These songs garnered millions of views on YouTube and propelled him into mainstream success.

Kodak continued to release successful albums such as "Painting Pictures" which debuted at number three on the US Billboard 200 chart and featured popular tracks like "Tunnel Vision" which peaked at number six on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart.

Despite some of his controversies, Kodak remains one of the most popular rappers in the industry today with an estimated net worth of $5 million. His music continues to resonate with fans all over the world who appreciate his unique style and delivery.
